## 9 The Role of Family and Friends in Shaping Your Habits

> The human mind knows how to get along with others. It wants to get along with others. This is our natural mode. You can override it—you can choose to ignore the group or to stop caring what other people think—but it takes work. Running against the grain of your culture requires extra effort.


[[humans are deeply social animals 202011031543 | Humans evolved to be sociable]] and as a consequence, our brains accustomed to getting along with other people. Most of the time it works to our advantage, but in other cases it doesn't.

Going against general knowledge is hard, it takes effort. But only when we learn to do that can we start to [[live life with intention 202010160226]]. We can't make reasoned decisions if we do what others consider good.

Another way to look at this is that you often need to [[the fastest way to grow is to step out of your comfort zone 202103202343 | step out of your comfort zone]].
