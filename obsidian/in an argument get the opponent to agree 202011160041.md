[[How to Win Friends and Influence People 202010270304]]

> His ([[Socrates]]) whole technique, now called the “[[Socratic Method]],” was based upon getting a “yes, yes” response. He asked questions with which his opponent would have to agree. He kept on winning one admission after another until he had an armful of yeses. He kept on asking questions until finally, almost without realizing it, his opponents found themselves embracing a conclusion they would have bitterly denied a few minutes previously.

When you are arguing with other people, try to get a "Yes" response from them. If you keep asking them question that yield a "Yes" response, you will make them more agreeable, making it more possible to win the whole argument. 

Even if the opponent does not agree, which is likely, you will get them closer towards your side. Psychologically, it is now much easier to agree with you on other points.

This is not possible, unless you prepare well. So, [[whenever you expect something to happen take a moment to prepare 202011160042]]
